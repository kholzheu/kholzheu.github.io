The Role of Workers in Knowledge Diffusion Across Firms

Authors: Anders Akerman; Kerstin Holzheu

Institution: Sciences Po

Date: November 2025 (posted December 2025)

Type: Working Paper

Abstract: We analyze the effect of labor mobility and innovation on productivity growth. We develop a multi-workerâ€“firm framework with random search and on-the-job mobility to estimate the relative contribution of mobility and R&D to growth. With event-study analysis based on shift-share international trade shocks, we show that the extent and direction of worker mobility affect firm productivity. Using Swedish microeconomic data, we find that over 44% of growth in average firm productivity can be attributed to worker mobility. Our results suggest that a slowdown of worker mobility, and more mobility from less productive firms, can depress growth in average firm productivity.
