The Role of Workers in Knowledge Diffusion Across Firms

Authors: Anders Akerman and Kerstin Holzheu

Version: December 2025

Abstract: We analyze the effect of labor mobility and innovation on productivity growth. We develop a multi-worker-firm framework with random search and on-the-job mobility to estimate the relative contribution of mobility and R\&D to growth. With event-study analysis based on shift-share international trade shocks, we show that extent and direction of worker mobility affect firm productivity. Using Swedish microeconomic data, we find that over 44\% of growth in average firm productivity can be attributed to worker mobility. Our results suggest that a slowdown of worker mobility, and more mobility from less productive firms, can depress growth in average firm productivity.
